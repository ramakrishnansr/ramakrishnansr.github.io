Bio

I am an Assistant Professor in the CSE department at UC Santa Cruz. My research uses empirical methods to detect, analyze and prevent large-scale threats to the security and privacy of Internet users.

I lead the RANDLab Research Group at UC Santa Cruz, where we work on a wide range of systems, networking, security, and privacy problems. My group builds empirical methods, systems, and data-driven analysis methods to study connection tampering attacks such as Internet censorship globally, offering insights into key network security events and the role of advanced network technology. We also build, analyze, and optimize network defense mechanisms, such as censorship circumvention and anonymity tools. My group also addresses key privacy issues that users and organizations alike face in today's online ecosystems. My research work has helped produce the Censored Planet Observatory, a global measurement platform for the continuous monitoring of various types of network interference.
